# ALBION MODEL GOVERNANCE DOCTRINE

## Core Principle

Albion is not a model company.

Albion is a governance, memory, routing, validation, and orchestration platform.

The intelligence layer is provider-agnostic.

## User Intelligence Layer

Users may connect any approved model provider, including but not limited to:

- OpenAI
- Anthropic
- Google Gemini
- xAI
- Local/self-hosted models
- Future providers

Users own:

- Model selection
- API keys
- Model costs
- Model preferences
- Privacy configuration

Albion does not require a specific model.

## Albion Layer

Albion provides:

- Memory
- Evidence Registry
- Routing Engine
- Truth Logic
- Governance
- Audit History
- Approval Workflows
- Council Review

These systems remain independent of any connected model.

Changing models must never affect:

- Memory
- Evidence
- Decisions
- Governance records
- Audit history
- Project state

## AI Council

The AI Council is the advisory frontier AI layer: ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude frontier models used by the Knights for planning, orchestration, objection, drift prevention, and route preparation.

The Council evaluates:

- Proposals
- Plans
- Architecture
- Risks
- Evidence
- Tradeoffs

The Council may recommend.

The Council may not self-authorize.

The Council does not hold final authority, merge authority, policy authority, governance authority, or execution authority.

## Human Authority

Roundtable human authority is final.

All execution authority originates from a human decision-maker.

The 3/3 law belongs to Roundtable as human authority, not to the AI Council.

AI may advise.

AI may challenge.

AI may validate.

AI may not override human authority.

## Strategic Position

Albion competes on:

- Better decisions
- Better governance
- Better memory
- Better validation
- Better project continuity

Albion does not compete on owning the best foundation model.

The platform becomes more valuable as external models improve.

## Long-Term Vision

Users bring intelligence.

Albion provides judgment, continuity, governance, and execution coordination.

The goal is not to produce the smartest answer.

The goal is to consistently produce the best outcome.
